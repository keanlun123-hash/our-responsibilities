Aftercare for Thoroughbreds

Our Responsibilities

Thoroughbred racehorses perform with maximum exertion, which has resulted in high accident rates and health problems such as bleeding from the lungs. For many horses, retirement from racing marks the beginning of a second career, which is likely to be for a longer duration than their initial racing career. As the majority of racehorses retire around 5 or 6 years old and have a life expectancy of over 20 years, supporting horses to have a long and fulfilling life after racing is an important consideration.
